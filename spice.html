Indian Spices

Sourced from the farms directly.Fresh, best quality products.

Indian spices are loved by people from all over the world. Spices are the real reason behind exotic flavours in Indian food. Indian cooksand households use a variety of spices for seasoning traditional Indian recipes. The popularity of Indian recipes has increased worldwide demand for Indian spices.

Almost 75% of spices used around the world are grown in and exported from the culturally rich country India. Indian spices not only have different tastes to feature in food but also they have medicinal value with no side effects.

India has an ideal climate for growing spices which is why it is the largest producer of spices in the world. Different kinds of spices grow in each region of India due to different climatic conditions across the country. Indian states Andhra Pradesh, Telangana, Kerala, Gujarat, Karnataka, Madhya Pradesh Maharashtra, Rajasthan, Tamil Nadu etc. contribute majorly to the spice production in India. Since ages spice export has played an important role in strengthening Indian economy.

Our products include a wide range of whole spices, ground spices, blended spices, curry powder and baking spices.

Some Famous Varieties
  • Turmeric:
    It is a spice which enriches your food with not only texture and colour but it also has medicinal features.It is used as a herb in medicines since almost 4000 years according to Ayurvedic records found.

  • Chilli powder:
    A punch of hot and tongue tingling taste is added to the meal by the spice.It also adds a nice color to the food.InIndia, most famous chillies(pepper) grow in States of Rajasthan and Madhya Pradesh.

  • Cumin:
    Cumin both as seeds or powder, has a nutty, peppery flavor.Simply by consuming cumin regularly, you can balance your blood sugar, control your cravings and watch any excess weight drop off.

  • Fennel seeds:
    The fennel seeds provide an anise-like aroma with fresh and sweet nuances. It is not only used as spice but also as natural mouth freshner.

  • Fenugreek Seeds (Methi Dana):
    Small brown seeds which have a pleasing flavor with a bitter undertone and a strong aroma reminiscent of curry powder. Fenugreek seeds are mostly used toasted, which enhances their acrid taste. Due to their powerful flavor, they are used in small amounts.

  • Coriander seeds:
    Coriander is one of the oldest of herbs and spices. The fruit of the coriander plant contains two seeds which, when dried, are the portions used as the dried spice. When ripe, the seeds are yellowish-brown or tan in colour with longitudinal ridges.
